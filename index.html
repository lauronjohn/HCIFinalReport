Summary

Our Real-Time Face Attendance System was developed to streamline attendance recording in academic and industrial settings, addressing the inefficiencies of traditional roll call or sign-in methods. Initial user research revealed common problems in existing attendance systems, such as time consumption and inaccuracies, leading to the motivation behind our solution. The system utilizes facial recognition technology to identify individuals quickly and accurately, marking their attendance in real-time.

The development process included designing and prototyping, where we used Ageitgey’s face recognition API for facial feature extraction and comparison with data stored in Firebase. User interaction with the system was straightforward, involving approaching the webcam, waiting for face detection, and automatic attendance marking. The physical design was informed by surveys and feedback from students and teachers, leading to a user-friendly interface.

Our evaluation through testing on university campus showed that the system was effective, user-friendly, and highly learnable, with positive feedback from participants. Looking ahead, we plan to enhance security features, implement 'express registration,' and conduct broader tests to ensure the system’s reliability and inclusivity across various user groups. This innovative approach marks a significant shift in everyday attendance recording procedures, enhancing efficiency and accuracy in both educational and professional environments.

User Research and Requirement Analysis

Motivation:

In many schools and workplaces, keeping track of who is present is usually done by hand or with simple computer systems. This old-fashioned way can lead to mistakes, take a lot of time, and be inefficient. People might sign in for their friends (called "buddy punching"), and it's a lot of work to keep track of everyone's attendance. Also, these methods don't give quick updates or detailed information about how often students or employees are actually there.

Solution:

Our Real-Time Face Attendance System is made to solve such common problem. It uses a camera to recognize people's faces to mark them as present. This makes the whole process faster, more accurate, and easier. It updates attendance records right away and keeps all the information in one place. This way, users such as teachers or managers can quickly see if someone is missing a lot of days and do something about it. For example, a school administrator might want to utilize our face attendance system so that they can accurately track and monitor student attendance to see who goes and doesn’t go to class. Similarly, a human resources manager in a company might want to utilize our system to automate employee attendance tracking and eliminate the need for time cards so that they can generate more accurate attendance reports for payroll purposes.

System and User Requirements:

  • Our system will accurately and efficiently recognize people’s faces to mark their attendance to ensure convenience and satisfaction for the end-users.
  • The system implements an intuitive and user-friendly interface that is easy to navigate for administrators, employees, or any users interacting with our system.
  • It stores the user’s personal data such as name, id, major, etc for front-end interface usage and feedback.
  • The system should generally be used in a professional environment such as a school, event venue, or a company, where attendance checking is critical.
  • It is responsive and provide immediate feedback to users when capturing facial features, recognizing faces, or logging attendance. In this way, it will help users confirm that the system is functioning correctly and provide assurance that attendance is being accurately recorded.
  • The types of technologies that our system should generally be run on is any computer system with a camera.

Design and Prototyping

(1) Conceptual Design and Development- How will the RFAS perform and how does it work?

The way our system implements facial recognition and attendance logging is, first, the system captures live video footage of individuals approaching the proximity of the environment and camera. Then, it detects and extracts the facial features from each frame of the video using computer vision algorithms in Ageitgey’s face recognition API. Once the facial features are extracted, the system compares them to Firebase (a real-time database) that stores the data of known faces. This comparison helps identify and recognize individuals in real-time. The system then logs the attendance of each recognized person.

Standard interaction procedure for users using our Face Attendance System:

  1. Program is initiated with the webcam turned on. The interface will display a status of “active” to indicate that the program is currently active and waiting to detect faces.
  2. User approaches the webcam
  3. User stays still and waits for the program to detect and process their face
  4. The green target will change color to red as an indication to the user that their face has been detected.
  5. A “loading” bar is displayed while the program compares their face to the known facial data stored in Firebase
  6. End-User is determined as “marked” and their attendance is recorded into the database.
  7. The user’s information as well as their updated attendance status is displayed on the program “marked” and “attended”.
  8. User exit the camera proximity

Evaluation and Results

Closely monitoring the effectiveness of our Real-Time Face Attendance System, we conducted a test on our university campus to evaluate its accuracy and user-friendliness in recording attendance. Three individuals, unfamiliar with our system, participated in this test. They were tasked with using the facial recognition technology at a specific location where our system's camera was set up.

Each participant approached the camera individually, activating the system, which then began the facial recognition process. We carefully observed how these first-time users interacted with the system, noting their ease of use and any potential challenges. Two team members were present during the test: one monitored the interaction and the other verified the data recorded in the real-time database.

The results were positive across all participants. The system accurately recognized each person and immediately updated their attendance status in the database, showcasing its efficiency. The participants found the process straightforward, reflecting the system's high learnability and minimal error rate. Their feedback emphasized the system's quick response and the simplicity of the process.

Encouraged by these promising initial results, we're planning more comprehensive tests with a larger and diverse group of users. These future tests aim to delve deeper into the system's performance in various scenarios and provide more extensive data on its overall usability and user experience.

Conclusion

In both academic and industrial environments, where attendance traditionally involves roll call or sign-in sheets, the adoption of our Real-Time Face Attendance System has revolutionized the process. Whether in college lectures or corporate settings, when individuals pass the camera, facial recognition technology efficiently and accurately logs their attendance. This system is particularly effective in managing large groups, ensuring prompt commencement of classes or business meetings. By leveraging advanced technology, the system not only simplifies the attendance recording but also improves accuracy. This modern approach is a significant enhancement to routine operations in educational institutions and workplaces alike, marking a positive shift in everyday procedures for students and professionals.

Future Works

Moving forward with our Real-Time Face Attendance System, we plan to enhance its security protocols and introduce a feature for 'express registration.' This would allow administrators or teachers to quickly enroll new users into the system by sending a secure link via email or text. Once the recipients click on this link, their basic information would be pre-registered, simplifying their first interaction with the facial recognition camera.

Additionally, we aim to conduct more comprehensive testing, involving participants from diverse backgrounds. This would help us assess the system’s performance and accuracy across a wider range of facial features and conditions, ensuring its reliability and inclusivity. Such improvements and expanded testing will not only fortify the system's security and ease of use but also broaden its applicability and effectiveness in various environments.
